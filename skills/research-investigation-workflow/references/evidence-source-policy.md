# Evidence Source Policy

Use this policy before searching, reading, saving, or citing material.

For substantive investigations, convert this policy into a SourceGuard discovery state before broad search. The policy tells the investigation what kind of evidence is allowed and needed; SourceGuard ranks the next search actions, records candidate sources, and preserves access gaps. SourceGuard planning is not factual proof.

## Source Classes

### Public Evidence

Public evidence is available through public web, public records, publications, public repositories, public datasets, press releases, court records, regulatory filings, or other open sources.

Rules:

- Search public sources only when browsing or public search is allowed.
- Use SourceGuard to rank public search directions when multiple leads, source roles, or counter-evidence needs compete for attention.
- Preserve URL, title, publisher, publication date, access date, and relevant excerpt or summary.
- Treat public availability as access, not as proof.
- Prefer primary sources for important claims.

### Local Evidence

Local evidence is available in the user's local files, workspace, notes, exports, logs, or downloaded artifacts.

Rules:

- Use only tools and paths available in the current environment.
- Do not assume local files are complete or current.
- Use SourceGuard to record which local paths, file classes, notes, logs, or exports were worth searching and which remained unsearched.
- Record path, timestamp, and extraction method.
- Ask before broad local searches if the scope is sensitive or unclear.

### Internal Evidence

Internal evidence belongs to private systems such as Drive, Gmail, Calendar, Slack exports, private repos, internal docs, or organization records.

Rules:

- Use only connected tools or explicit user-provided access.
- Use SourceGuard to separate allowed internal search actions from access-gated actions before querying private systems.
- Keep internal content out of reusable public summaries by default.
- Record internal source ids or paths rather than duplicating sensitive content in the History Ledger.
- Label internal evidence in the source appendix.

### Permission-Gated Evidence

Permission-gated evidence is plausible but unavailable because access, subscription, credentials, legal permission, or user authorization is missing.

Rules:

- Record an access gap.
- Feed the access gap back into SourceGuard so dependent leads are either rerouted to substitute evidence or downgraded.
- Do not infer the missing content.
- Do not cite the missing source as support.
- Downgrade claims that depend on gated material.

### Hypothesis / Inference

Hypothesis is a possible explanation, lead, or inference that is not direct evidence.

Rules:

- Label as hypothesis, inference, or candidate explanation.
- Link it to the evidence that motivated it.
- Use SourceGuard to search for direct, limiting, or falsifying evidence before promoting a hypothesis.
- Never write it as confirmed fact.
- Move it to findings only after support is strong enough and LogicGuard audit passes.

## Evidence Handling Rules

- A source is not evidence by itself.
- Evidence is not an event by itself.
- An event is not a trace by itself.
- A trace is not a final claim by itself.
- Motive, incentives, constraints, expert commentary, and market concern are context or interpretation unless the model links them to concrete execution, causality, or outcome evidence.
- Local, scope-limited, or actor-specific evidence does not support broader-scope claims unless a bridging source creates that link.
- Confidence language must follow evidence strength and claim-boundary review.
- Contradictions must stay visible until resolved or explicitly scoped.

## General Evidence Roles

For substantive investigations, classify each important source by role:

```text
claim origin or rumor source
direct/original fact record
source statement or actor claim
scope or boundary evidence
execution, outcome, or impact evidence
context, motive, or constraint evidence
expert, media, or analyst interpretation
counter, limiting, or non-occurrence evidence
historical background
future hypothesis trigger
```

Use SourceGuard to track which roles are covered, which are only candidate-covered, and which still need search. Missing counter, limiting, execution, stakeholder, or future-trigger roles should produce a new search action or an explicit downgrade.

Execution or outcome evidence includes items such as implementation records, observed behavior, transaction or usage data, follow-up decisions, affected-stakeholder evidence, enforcement or operating records, measurement data, or later outcome records. If key signals are absent, state which were checked before writing a negative or partial finding.

Counter or limiting evidence includes contrary sources, failed searches with strong coverage, scope limits, non-occurrence records, affected-party silence that is itself evidenced, methodological caveats, and sources showing that an interpretation remains only a hypothesis.

## Source Freshness

For time-sensitive topics, record:

- source date;
- coverage period;
- access date;
- whether the source is primary, secondary, or commentary;
- whether the source is current enough for the claim it supports;
- whether a later source supersedes or narrows it;
- whether newer evidence may stale the conclusion.

Do not force a timeline section unless time boundaries matter to the conclusion.
